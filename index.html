Current Projects

Student Researcher @ UC Santa Cruz Science Internship Program  Jun 2023—Aug 2023

Interned for ten weeks in the University of California, Santa Cruz Science Internship Program under the guidance of graduate researcher Pooneh Safayenikoo & Professor Andrew Quinn. I researched how the brain makes decisions and how that can be represented with an artificial neural network. Furthermore, I familiarized myself with neural network calculations like forward pass, gradient descent, and backpropagation. Using these skills, I was able to utilize the MNIST dataset and implement my own neural network from scratch using Python. After reading multiple research papers, my research group and I created a final presentation which was presented live to UCSC Professors and other interns.

Student Researcher @ University of Cincinnati College of Pharmacy  Jan 2023—Present

Assisting postdoctoral researcher Bruna da Silva Soley with a melanoma research project to understand the mechanism by which melanoma cells evade treatment agents and gain drug resistance. For my role in the lab, I have been doing various molecular and cellular biology experiments, including cell culture, western blotting, genotyping by PCR, and microscopic imaging. I also help with reagent preparation, lab management, and lab cleanup. When needed, I read relevant scientific literature to further my understanding. In October 2023, I jointly presented the findings at the University of Cincinnati Cancer Metastasis Research Symposium.

Independent Research @ UC San Diego  Jan 2022—May 2022

Particpated in a neurobiology mentorship under Dr. Robert Clark, studying various topics in neuroscience, such as basic concepts and terminology in memory, levels of processing, interference effects, mass and spacing effects, imagery, chunking, and mnemonic aids to memory. I read multiple research papers on the physiology of memory, including fear memory, frontal lobes and working memory, and classical conditioning. At the end of the mentorship, I created and presented a slideshow to Dr. Clark discussing fear conditioning as well as recent and remote memory. Using the information I learned, I wrote a review paper on the organization of memory and recent understandings of amnesia. This paper was published in the Journal of Student Research in August 2022.
